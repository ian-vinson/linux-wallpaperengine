в—Ҹ This is very revealing. The fragment shader does a manual perspective divide:
  texCoord = v_ScreenCoord.xy/v_ScreenCoord.z * 0.5 + 0.5, while gl_Position
  (the actual raster extent) is driven purely by a_TexCoord, always covering the
  full destination canvas. This means composelayer's technique is: rasterize a
  full-screen quad, but sample the source scene buffer at the NDC position that
  the object's real geometry (a_Position via MVP) would project to. If that
  projected quad is larger than the Вұ1 NDC range (rather than snapping exactly
  to it), the affine interpolation will read a correct value near the canvas
  center but overshoot [0,1] toward the edges вҖ” exactly the observed "valid
  center, clamped-streak border" pattern. This is a concrete, falsifiable
  theory. Let me verify it and find where the over-scaling could originate.
